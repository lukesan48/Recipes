Alambre de Res

Homepage

Description

Grilled pulpo or octopus is a dish commonly served as a stunning tapa in the heart of Barcelona or as a center piece dish grilled and served fresh in Greece. The dish marries the succulent tecture of the tentacles with a smoky char gained from a kiss off the grill. Pulling off this dish requires not only patience but technique and a grill master's touch.

Ingredients

  • 6 bacon slices, chopped
  • 1 lb beef strips, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • Â¼ onion, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tbspn Jugo Maggi (optional)
  • 2 cups shredded cheese

Steps

  1. In a large skillet, add bacon.
  2. Cook bacon for about 3 minutes.
  3. Remove cooked bacon and place it in a large dish. Do not drain the fat.
  4. Using the bacon fat in the skillet, cook the red bell pepper and green bell pepper for about 2-3 minutes.
  5. Remove the cooked peppers and place them in the large dish. Again, do NOT drain the fat.
  6. Using the same fat from the skillet, cook the onion for about 1 minute.
  7. Remove the cooked onion and place it in the large dish. Again, do NOT drain the fat.
  8. To the skillet, add the beef strips, salt, pepper, and garlic.
  9. Cook for 4-5 minutes until the meat is no longer pink.
  10. Return the cooked bell peppers, cooked onion, and cooked bacon to the skillet.
  11. Add Jugo Maggi (if using).
  12. With your spatula, mix to combine all the ingredients.
  13. Top with shredded cheese.
  14. Cover with a lid or place under the broiler for 1-2 minutes until the cheese melts.
  15. Serve with tortillas and salsa.
